Information content programming exercise.

In addition to building on your programming skills, this exercise tests your understanding of the entropy concepts that have been covered in class. The goal of the exercise is to write a Python program that does the following (grading shown in red italics ):

  1. Read in a multiple sequence alignment in Stockholm format (you may assume the file is correctly formatted); (10)
    Relevant points of the Stockholm format for this homework:
    1. Lines starting with #'s are comments, and should be ignored
    2. The amount of whitespace between fields is arbitrary
    3. On lines with information, you have optional whitespace, name, whitespace, sequence
    4. Gaps may be specified as a period ( . ) or a dash ( - )
    5. You can assume that the entire sequence is presented on a single line, instead of split over multiple lines (though they can technically legally be split in Stockholm format).
    6. The relevant part of the file ends with //

Do not print any of the information you just calculated in your final submission (though you'll definitely want to while debugging!). Instead, add the following functionality to your program:

  1. Store the generated entropy and mutual information values for each column or pair of columns. (note: since you should not return columns!)
